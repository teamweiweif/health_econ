# Rejected Designs

Created: 2026-07-07

## Rejected at Setup

| Design or choice | Status | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Pure treated-versus-untreated DiD | Rejected | The 2022 CMS reporting and rating changes were national. Medicare/Medicaid-certified nursing homes were exposed to the reporting regime, so lower-exposure facilities can serve only as comparison facilities, not untreated controls. |
| 2024 federal minimum staffing rule as main exposure | Rejected | The research objective is the 2022 transparency and Five-Star rating reporting shocks. The 2024 rule is later policy context and may contaminate late post-period interpretation. |
| Causal ML as core design | Rejected for main analysis | The project is a panel policy transparency/rating paper. ML would be secondary only if a real targeting question emerges after credible main estimates. |
| Resident-level clinical outcomes | Rejected | Public CMS data used here do not provide resident-level clinical records. Facility-level deficiencies and ratings can be analyzed, but not overclaimed as resident health effects. |
